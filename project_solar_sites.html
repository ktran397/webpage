Project Type: Suitability Analysis

Overview

This project evaluated potential solar farm sites near Saddle Lake by comparing multiple spatial factors that affect development feasibility. The goal was to identify the strongest location based on terrain, energy potential, and infrastructure access.

Methods

  • Assessed candidate sites using terrain and cost-related criteria.
  • Compared site size and development potential across the study area.
  • Considered access to infrastructure as part of the final evaluation.
  • Used mapping outputs to support the final decision-making process.

Outcome

The project narrowed down which site offered the best balance of efficiency and practicality for solar panel development. The final map communicates both the spatial reasoning and the tradeoffs behind the recommended site.
